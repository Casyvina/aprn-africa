Executive Summary

In 33 days — from May 3 to June 5, 2026 — APRN (African Pipeline Resource Network) has been built from a concept into a fully operational pan-African pipeline institution. What began as a WhatsApp message from Lucy Okeke to Joseph Agwuh has become Africa's first dedicated pipeline research, training, and advocacy network.

The website is live at aprn-africa.org with 27+ pages, a full member dashboard, Sanity CMS, Supabase backend, and an AI content generator. Strategic documents — presentation, research brief, competitive intelligence, brand guidelines, membership structure, employment letters — are all delivered and in active use. PTC Berlin (the world's premier pipeline conference) has confirmed interest in partnership. EITEP Institute Germany is engaged. CAC registration is in progress.

This report captures every deliverable, every decision, every milestone, and every pending item as of June 5, 2026.

Lucy confirms EITEP Institute Germany (eitep-training.com) as APRN's international training partner. EITEP runs certified pipeline courses globally but has no Africa presence — APRN is the Africa delivery vehicle. First APRN presentation drafts begin.

Presentation v1–v5 · Research Brief · Database
Full-stack research and document production sprint. Pipeline research brief, 14-slide APRN presentation (multiple versions), competitive intelligence report v1, and pipeline industry database covering Nigeria operators, contractors, engineers, and regulators.

Joseph's Engineering Background Confirmed
Joseph informs Lucy he studied Civil Engineering for 5 years — making his work on APRN not just technical support but sector-informed engineering research. Title updated to "Civil Engineer & Digital Lead" and later to "Head, Technology & Innovation".

Brand Guidelines · Competitive Intelligence v1–v5
Official APRN Brand Guidelines document built (Navy #001B44, Gold #D4A017). Competitive Intelligence Report expanded to cover PLAN, NSE, COREN, SPE Nigeria, EITEP, AEC. NIPITECS 2026 resolution added. Mobile responsive styling applied.
11 MAY 2026
WIMEE Africa · APLS Morocco · Logo Received
Lucy confirms 3 major naming decisions: "Women in Pipeline" → WIMEE Africa (Women in Midstream Energy and Engineering). "Pipeline Conference" → African Pipeline Leadership Summit (APLS). Conference location: Morocco (NMGP corridor terminus). Official APRN logo received.

Employment Letters Signed · Joseph's Title Confirmed
Two formal appointment letters issued: Joseph Agwuh as Director, Applied Engineering & Innovation (later aligned to Head, Technology & Innovation per website). Allison Gabriel as APRN Youth Ambassador. CAC registration initiated.

NCDMB Added · Presentation v10 · CV Built

Full website built on Next.js 16 + Supabase + Sanity + Tailwind v4. 27 pages deployed. Member dashboard, auth flow, Sanity CMS, AI content generator (Claude API → Sanity), admin panel, onboarding wizard. Paystack integrated (test keys).

First official APRN team meeting on Zoom. Attendees: Lucy Okeke (Founder), Joseph Agwuh (Head, Technology & Innovation), Allison Gabriel (Youth Ambassador), Pieter-Bas Nederveen (Advisory Committee). 30-minute introduction session.
